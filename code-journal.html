Code Journal

One year ago I had never coded and thought Ruby was only a precious stone. However, I was tired of my job and felt like it was a dead-end. Looking around for new jobs, I found that many of the jobs that sounded interesting required some knowledge of programming languages. This is what started me on my journey to Ada.

The first six weeks of Ada were devoted to learning Ruby. It is an understatement to say that I felt like I was being firehosed. Ada is a demanding and fast-paced program...doing it with a 1 year old has felt like Niagra Falls. Somehow, I survived the first unit of Ruby with a somewhat slippery grasp of classes.

Our last week was dedicated to learning HTML and CSS speed-dating style. It has been a nice change of scenery after Ruby and before Ruby on Rails. The extent of my knowledge in this subject is displyed here, in this website.

On Monday we start Ruby on Rails. To me, Ruby on Rails just sounds like Ruby, but faster (wasn't Niagra Falls of Ruby fast enough?). I realize this is not at all what it means, so I'm eager to find out what it really is!
