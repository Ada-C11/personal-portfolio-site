About Me

I'm Shubha Rajan (pronounced ʃʊbʱaː raːdʒən) I use "she" and "they" pronouns.

I'm a software-engineer-in-training, writer, designer, educator and Americorps Alum. I'm from Southern California, but I currently live in Seattle, WA and attend Ada Developers Academy full-time. I also volunteer at Seattle Makers where I help members troubleshoot machines and work on shop projects.

I also am part of Free Radicals, an activist collective of scientists that aims to hold STEM institutions accountable for damage they cause to marginalized communities. I previously helped FreeRads revamp their website and am currently collaborating on creating a Radical Science education toolkit. I am passionate about promoting creativity and new ideas, as well as connecting innovative ideas and resources with those who need them the most.

Things I do other than work:

  • Write comedy for the stage and occasionally perform.
  • Make my own board games and one-page-one-shot RPGs (think D&D but way shorter and sillier).
  • Take pictures with my trusty Nikon.
