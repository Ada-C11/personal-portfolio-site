About me

Why didn’t I get into software development sooner? It checks so many boxes for me: it’s challenging, collaborative, the applications are endless, and it rewards both my logical and my social sides. The lack of diversity in this field repelled me for a long time, but the opportunity to join the unique environment of Ada combined with the shrinking job market for translators gave me the extra push I needed.

But I am also happy for the experience I bring from my life before Ada. I graduated in International Relations from one of the top universities in Brazil and went on to work at the national transportation department in my country, where I helped build connections between members of Congress and technical experts.

When I left Brazil, I spent some time exploring the field of technical communication, which was when I had my first taste of programming languages. I was hooked, but I had to set this interest aside for about three years as I worked with software localization, testing, and translation while pursuing a certificate in translation from the University of Toronto - Extension.

As for life after Ada, I would love to work on logistics issues, improve developer's workflow, write awesome documentation, and help bring more folks from diverse backgrounds into tech.
